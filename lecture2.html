Institutions

What is an institution?

"An institution is an organization with a set of rules and practices that inform its members about their relationships with one another and how they should interact. Institutions may be formal, with written rules, or they may be informal." (Rom, Hidaka, and Walker 2022, p. 28)

Examples? Schools, families, religions, criminal gangs, etc.

Political institutions: Government agencies, local councils, parliaments, political parties, political movements, NGO's, media, international org. etc.)

Public policy

The output/result/product of politics

"All the actions that governments take that are designed to influence individual, group, institutional, or national behavior."

(Non-exhaustive) list of examples:

  • Laws
  • Other regulations
  • Executive orders
  • Rulings
  • Public funding

In short, it includes any decision made by a government or other official authority that attempts to guide human behaviour.

Institutions and public policy

Many different theories about which factors shape and determine public policy, including power, public opinion, economy, and institutions.

So (at least partly) we can think of public policies as the output produced by institutions.

But at the same time public policy also affects and potentially change or create new institutions.

Institutions ↔ public policy
