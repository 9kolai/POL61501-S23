Cases for your case studies

For your presentations (50% of final grade) and exam papers (50%), you will need to:

  • Find a case to analyse.
  • And use theories covered in class to answer some questions about your case.

So it is a good idea to start thinking about cases you might be interested in. But what do we mean by cases?

What is a case and what is it a case of?

Fundamentally, we can imagine two types of cases that would be relevant for your projects:

  • Regulations (with relevance to your field of engineering)
    • A specific standard, rule, norm, or regulation.
    • Explain how and why this regulation was established.
    • ...using theories from class to offer one or alternative explanations to this quesiton.
  • Engineering projects (where politics plays/played a significant role)
    • Fx. public infrastructure projects, or private projects that require(d) political approval.
    • Or public policy decisions that affect engineering projects (fx. banning cars running on fossil fuel, or political support for specific engineering solutions such as carbon capture)

Voting in your home country

Take a couple of minutes to find the answers to the following questions:

  • Who are allowed to vote in your country?
  • What was the turnout at the last general election in your home country?
  • Is this a fall or increase in turnout compared to previously?

Discussion

Do you think voting is an effective way to influence politics in your home country?

How to particiapte in politics?

What is the purpose of citizen participation in a democracy?

How can/do citizens participate in politics (other than voting)?

  • Party membership
  • Contact a politician (elected representative)
  • Sign petitions
  • Demonstrations and protests
  • Consumerism (boycots and buycots)
  • Follow the (political) news
  • Share and like political messages online
  • Civil disobedience

Group work

For each type of political pariticpation discuss the following questions:

  • Is it expsensive?
  • Can it be done anywhere? At any time?
  • Can it be done alone or in groups?
  • Does it require special knowledge or information?
  • Does it require any special access?
  • Is it dangerous?
  • What might stop someone from engaging in this activity?

Based on your discussions try to rank the methods of particiaption from least to most accessible.

Discussion

  • Which factors might affect political participation?
    • What are the main barriers to participation?
    • What would be most important to encourage more particpation? (money, time, group support)
  • What do you think it means for democracy that/if participation is not equal?
  • Do you beleive political participation is important for democracy to function? Any type of participation?
